## Purpose

Describe how the network is segmented, how traffic flows between segments, and where the trust boundaries sit.

## Scope

Logical design. Physical wiring and per-port assignments belong in [Inventory > Networks](/inventory/networks).

## Current bridges

| Bridge | Node | Role | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| `vmbr0` | `pve1`, `pve3` | Management / trusted LAN on `192.168.1.0/24` | Both Proxmox management IPs sit here; gateway `192.168.1.1` |
| `vmbr1` | `pve3` | Routed internal network, host IP `10.0.0.124` | Carries `opnsense.net1`; CIDR pending validation |
| `vmbr2` | `pve3` | Holds `192.168.1.112`, currently down | Purpose pending validation — keep, repurpose, or remove |

### Edge

The OPNsense VM (`401`) on `pve3` is dual-homed:
- `net0` on `vmbr0` — management/trusted LAN side.
- `net1` on `vmbr1` — routed internal side.

OPNsense owns WAN termination and inter-zone policy.

### Ingress

User-facing HTTPS ingress goes through Nginx Proxy Manager on `pve1` (VM `100`). The `cloudflared` LXC on `pve1` provides outbound tunneling for selected services. A secondary Nginx instance (VM `105`) exists; its target role is pending confirmation — keep one, retire the other.

## Target zoning

The intent is to consolidate around the following logical zones, with OPNsense enforcing the boundaries.

| Zone | Purpose | Status |
|------|---------|--------|
| Management / admin | Proxmox hosts, OPNsense management, infra console access | Confirmed on `192.168.1.0/24` |
| Services | Platform and application workloads exposed via ingress | Pending validation |
| Storage | NFS, TrueNAS, replication traffic | Pending validation |
| Lab | Isolated pre-production and test workloads on `pve3` | Pending validation |
| WAN / edge | Internet uplink behind OPNsense | Owned by OPNsense |

VLAN IDs and CIDRs for the non-management zones are **pending design confirmation**. Do not record values here until they are agreed and applied on the switches and on OPNsense.

## Rules

- Default-deny between zones. Inter-zone access is allow-listed on OPNsense.
- The management plane is reachable only from the management zone.
- Ingress to user-facing services traverses Nginx Proxy Manager (or the cloudflared tunnel where explicitly chosen); direct exposure of backend services is not allowed.

## Notes

- `vmbr2` and its `192.168.1.112` assignment need a documented purpose or removal.
- Cluster replication traffic shares `vmbr0` today. Promoting it to a dedicated storage zone is part of the [migration plan](/architecture/migration-plan).
