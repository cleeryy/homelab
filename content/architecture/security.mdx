## Trust boundaries

- **WAN** — terminated by OPNsense (VM `401`) on `pve3`. WAN policy is defined inside OPNsense and is not exported into this documentation.
- **Management LAN** — `192.168.1.0/24` on `vmbr0`. Proxmox hosts, Docker hosts, ingress VMs, and the NAS all sit on this segment.
- **Internal / routed** — `10.0.0.0/24` (at least) on `vmbr1`, reached through OPNsense `net1` on `pve3`.

The current segmentation is binary at the bridge level (management LAN vs. internal/routed). VLANs and finer-grained zones are not confirmed.

## Ingress

- HTTPS termination is handled by reverse-proxy VMs on `pve1`:
  - `ubuntu-nginx-proxy-manager` (`100`).
  - `ubuntu-nginxrpm` (`105`).
- A `cloudflared` LXC (`103`) on `pve1` provides outbound tunneling for selected services.

The split between the two reverse-proxy VMs is not confirmed and is a known piece of ambiguity in the ingress path.

## Remote access

- `apache-guacamole` (LXC `106`) on `pve3` is the visible remote-access gateway.

## Egress

- `cloudflared` (LXC `103`) — tunnel egress for selected services.
- Several Docker stacks include `gluetun` (VPN client) at the compose level:
  - `Servarr` host.
  - `QBittorrent` host.
- The exact services routed through `gluetun` are managed inside each compose stack and not captured here.

## Known operational observations

- `gluetun` on the `QBittorrent` host was reported as **unhealthy** at the time of the last observation. This is a transient state, not a configuration finding, but worth flagging.

## Out of scope for this page

- Identity, account, secret, and credential management: not exported into this documentation.
- Per-service TLS configuration: see each service page.
- WAN-side firewall rules: live in OPNsense.
